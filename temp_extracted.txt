What it is &amp; How it works

BIO NPK is a multi-microbial biofertilizer that improves availability of Nitrogen, Phosphorus and Potassium in the root zone.Each microorganism plays a specific role:

Azospirillum fixes atmospheric nitrogen

PSB converts insoluble phosphorus into plant-available form

KMB mobilizes fixed soil potassium

Together, they enhance root development, nutrient uptake efficiency and overall crop performance.




Key Benefits

Enhances early root development, leading to stronger plant establishment and higher yield

Improves efficiency of applied potassium fertilizers and optimizes water use

Increases available phosphorus levels, boosting overall plant growth

Improves crop growth, yield and produce quality

Supports sustainable nutrition by reducing dependence on chemical fertilizers




Primary Use (USP)

Complete biological NPK nutrition through live microbial activity

At seed treatment stage

Before transplanting

During early vegetative growth

As a basal soil application

Plantation and field crops

Mix 10 ml SUN BIO NPK in cold jaggery solution

Mix 10 ml SUN BIO NPK in 1 litre water

ZN-Mob- Zinc Nutrient Activation Bio Fertilizer

Contents (Table of Contents)

Zinc Solubilising Bacteria (CFU: 1 x 10⁸ cells/ml)(Biological agent that converts insoluble soil zinc into plant-available zinc)




What it is &amp; How it works

Zn-Mob is a biological zinc mobilizer formulated with zinc-solubilizing bacteria that stay active in the root zone. These microbes produce organic acids that break down insoluble zinc compounds (such as zinc sulphides, zinc oxide and zinc carbonate) into Zn⁺ ions that plants can absorb. This improves zinc uptake without relying on chemical zinc fertilizers.




Key Benefits

Improves zinc availability in soil and root zone for better uptake.

Enhances photosynthesis, carbohydrate metabolism &amp; enzyme activation (zinc-related processes).

Supports seed production and hormone biosynthesis through improved micronutrient balance.

Reduces dependence on chemical zinc fertilizers and provides residual benefits to subsequent crops.

Increases soil microbial population and overall soil health.




Primary Use (USP)

Biological zinc mobilizer that converts fixed soil zinc into plant-available form through microbial activity.




When to Use

At sowing/transplanting for zinc correction from day one.

During early vegetative growth when zinc demand is high.

When zinc deficiency symptoms are visible (chlorosis, stunting). 




Crops

Suitable for:

Cereals &amp; millets

Fruits &amp; vegetables

Flowers &amp; ornamentals

Sugarcane

Plantation &amp; field crops




Application &amp; Dosage

Mode of application (soil + fertigation + drenching):

Soil Application (Per Acre):Mix 1 litre of Zn-Mob with 50–100 kg well decomposed manure/cake and apply evenly on moist soil.

Drenching:Mix 5–10 ml in 1 litre of water and apply near root zone.

Fertigation (Per Acre):Mix 1–2 litres in sufficient water and apply in root zone through drip system.

N-Azos- Enhanced Nitrogen root Activator

Contents (Table of Contents)



Nitrogen Fixing Bacteria Azospirillum Bacteria (CFU: 1 X 108 Cells / ml)



What it is &amp; How it works

N-Azos contains beneficial Azospirillum bacteria that colonize the plant root zone and fix atmospheric nitrogen into a plant-available form. The bacteria remain active in soil, support root development, and improve nutrient availability throughout the crop cycle.




Key Benefits

Reduces chemical nitrogen fertilizer requirement by up to 30%

Fixes approximately 15–20 kg nitrogen per hectare

Improves root growth and overall plant vigour

Enhances soil microbial activity and soil fertility

Supports higher yield and better crop quality



Primary Use (USP)

N-Azos is primarily used as a biological nitrogen fixer that naturally enriches the soil, enhancing plant growth and reducing dependency on chemical nitrogen fertilizers.




When to Use

Apply in nitrogen-deficient soils to supply natural nitrogen.

Use during early growth stages of vegetables, pulses, cereals, and other crops.

Ideal when aiming to increase yield without increasing chemical fertilizer use.

Apply to soils with low microbial activity to boost beneficial nitrogen-fixing microbes.






Crops

Vegetables, fruits, cereals, pulses, oilseeds, flowers, sugarcane, plantation crops.




Application &amp; Dosage

Seed TreatmentMix 10 ml in cold jaggery water, coat seeds uniformly, shade-dry, and sow the same day.

Seedling Root DipMix 10 ml per litre of water. Dip roots for 5–10 minutes before transplanting.

Soil ApplicationMix 1 litre per acre with well-decomposed compost/FYM and apply evenly on moist soil.

Soil DrenchingDilute 5–10 ml per litre of water and apply near the root zone.

Drip / FertigationApply 1–2 litres per acre through drip irrigation.

2️⃣ Causes of Nitrogen deficiency

Nitrogen deficiency usually happens due to:

🌾 Soil-related causes:

Low organic matter in soil 

Poor microbial activity 

Sandy soils (leaching losses) 

Continuous chemical fertilizer use (microbial death) 

💧 Environmental causes:

Heavy rainfall → nitrogen leaching 

Waterlogging → denitrification losses 

High temperature → faster nitrogen loss 

🚫 Farming practices:

No use of biofertilizers 

Imbalanced fertilization (only P &amp; K focus) 

Over-dependence on urea → long-term depletion

Key Benefits

Fixes up to ~80% of nitrogen requirement in crops like sugarcane

Acts like “black urea,” enhancing nitrogen supply naturally

Solubilizes insoluble phosphorus to improve nutrient availability

Promotes growth and can increase cane yield by up to 10%

Supports higher sugar recovery (≈ +0.5%) in sugarcane

Primary Use (USP)

N-Acteo is primarily used as a liquid bio-fertilizer that enhances soil microbial activity, improves nutrient availability, and promotes vigorous plant growth.




When to Use

Apply in nutrient-deficient soils to improve microbial activity and nutrient cycling.

Use during early growth stages of vegetables, fruits, and field crops.

Ideal when you want to enhance soil fertility and crop vigor naturally.

Apply to soils with low organic matter to stimulate beneficial microbes.

What it is &amp; How it works

Gypsum (calcium sulfate) is a natural soil amendment widely used for sodic soil reclamation and sulphur fertilization. When applied to soil, it forms calcium-rich soil particles that exchange with sodium ions in sodic soils, helping leach sodium away with irrigation or rain. In sulphur-deficient soils, gypsum supplies plant-available sulphur, supporting essential metabolic functions. Gypsum does not significantly change soil pH in non-sodic soils but improves soil structure and nutrient mobility when properly incorporated and leached into the root zone.
